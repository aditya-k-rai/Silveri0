                   Introduction and Aim of the Project

1.1 Introduction

Today, health organizations are incorporating automated and intelligent software to replace
manual processes in handling, recording, and administering patients. The efficient management
of the resources within the hospital plays a crucial role within the healthcare system. This is
because the delivery of prompt and accurate information concerning appointments, availability
of physicians, prescriptions, and billings among others, to patients and other hospital personnel
is extremely vital.

UniCare Hospital Management System, henceforth called UniCare HMS, is a smart, cloud-
enabled, web-based solution intended to help digitize and automate all processes involved in a
hospital. In the past, hospitals were operating using manual processes for appointment booking,
keeping patient details manually, and having separate databases for different departments. These
practices often resulted in data redundancy, bureaucracy, medical mistakes, and even delay in
providing healthcare services.

For its backend, the system employs the Node.js and Express.js framework. This makes the
system scalable and responsive in real time. The Symptom Checker application is powered by
Google Gemini AI, and it uses patient symptom reports to predict possible diseases, alongside
suggesting the best department to visit at the hospital. Data is stored on the MySQL relational
database management system. Email notifications are sent automatically using the Nodemailer
module.

1.2 Motivation of the Project

The main reason behind the conception of this project UniCare HMS is inefficiencies caused by
using manual systems for hospital management. In today's hectic healthcare industry, there is
loss of time on behalf of both patients as well as employees because of paperwork, scheduling,
and disjointed record keeping. Furthermore, UniCare HMS was also prompted by the
revolutionary impact of artificial intelligence on the healthcare sector. Using the AI-powered
symptom analyzer, the system not only converts paper-based files into electronic format but
also offers clinical intelligence through it.

1.3 Problem Statement

Management of hospitals in the traditional way faces many challenges, such as the following:

    · Data Inconsistency: Medical files, medications, and other information of patients are
         saved separately from each other.

7
    · Appointment Scheduling Through Physical Visits: Patients are required to physically
         visit the hospital for appointment bookings.

    · Real-Time Accessibility Issues: Employees can't immediately verify the availability of
         doctors, beds, or rooms.

    · Missing Automated Notifications: Patients do not get automated reminders regarding
         their upcoming appointment schedules, causing frequent no-shows.

    · Poor Analytical Tools: Hospital management does not have any dashboard tools for
         assessing the inflow of patients, income trends, or doctor productivity.

1.4 Project Purpose

Overall, UniCare HMS seeks to develop an integrated platform for hospital management that
will ensure precise and immediate dissemination of data to all parties involved ­ patients,
doctors, nurses, and administrative staff ­ via a secure web portal and automatic e-mail
notification.

1.5 Objectives

The project consists of the following goals:

    · Multi-Role Secure System: Design an application that will include roles for
         Administrators, Doctors, Nurses, and Patients with proper authentication and access
         privileges.

    · Symptom-Based Analysis with AI: Incorporate the Google Gemini API to provide
         dynamic analysis of symptoms provided by patients and suggest suitable departments.

    · Notification System: Utilize Nodemailer to automate notification processes such as
         appointment confirmation, reminder, and billing receipts.

    · Data Storage and Management: Create a normalized MySQL database for data storage
         such as patient, doctor profile, appointment, prescription, and billing details.

    · User Interface: Design a business professional user interface responsive for desktop,
         tablet, and mobile devices.

1.6 Project Scope

The UniCare HMS has the following scopes:
    · Tech scope: The platform is built using MEEN Stack (MySQL, Express.js, Gemini AI,
         Node.js) and RESTful API design architecture.

    · Function Scope ­ Admin: Full CRUD functionalities for patients, doctors, departments,
         bed management, billing, and analytics dashboard.

    · Function Scope ­ Doctor: Access to assigned appointments, updating of patient
         records, and issuing electronic prescriptions.

    · Functional Scope - Patient: Schedule appointments, review patient records, AI-based
         symptom checker, and retrieve invoices.

    · Security Scope: Session management, password hashing using bcrypt, and sanitization
         to protect against SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS).

                  Background Study and Research Gap

2.1 Background to this Study

In recent years, there has been a major revolution within hospital and healthcare administration
systems. From the perspective of technology, the change has been from manual registers to
digital registers. Despite this, the core aim has been consistent in that it is all about providing
the appropriate medical data to the correct stakeholders at the right time. This, however, has not
been the case for most hospitals, particularly those operating in tier-2 cities and hospitals
affiliated to schools.

2.2 History of Higher Education Communication

Historically, the development of hospital management systems is marked by different eras,
including:

    · The Manual Era: Where patient information would be kept in physical registers,
         booking logs, and handwritten prescription slips. Loss of data, illegible handwriting, and
         data duplication were very frequent problems.

    · The Early Computerized Era: Desktop applications that were used to handle billing
         processes or manage pharmacology, operating independently of other departments
         within the hospital.

    · The Internet Age: The advent of websites for patient booking was introduced, although
         limited to scheduling, and not integrated with patient records and AI technologies.

2.3 Research on the Technology: Stack Choice

For order to implement a modern solution, extensive research was conducted regarding the
technological state. The choice of using MEEN Stack architecture (MySQL, Express, Node.js)
and AI integration was made on the grounds of the following research.

2.3.1 Research into Node.js and Express
According to the research conducted on back-end technology, it has been revealed that Node.js,
using Google's V8 engine for executing the JavaScript language, is wholly compatible for
developing real-time applications. Being a non-blocking and event-driven system, Node.js can
handle multiple requests from doctors and patients simultaneously without any delays. This
becomes very essential especially during peak hours like morning OPD rush.

10
2.3.2 Relational Database (MySQL)
Even though the trend seems to point towards the usage of NoSQL databases, there has been a
lot of emphasis placed on MySQL from an academic perspective when it comes to healthcare
information structures. Information pertaining to medical files, patient records, schedules of
doctors, billing, etc. is well-structured and connected. MySQL ensures that ACID properties
allow for immediate consistency.

2.3.3 Large Language Models (LLMs)
One of the key themes explored through literature review is the use of artificial intelligence in
health care triage. Studies have shown that patients usually have difficulties in determining the
specialist they should seek for their particular symptoms. The Google Gemini Pro API is able
to understand human language well enough to be able to interpret the symptoms expressed in
English and find possible medical illnesses and the departments that should be consulted.

2.4 Research Gap

Even with the presence of some digital technologies, the following critical gaps still exist in
hospital management:

The following gaps have been identified:

    · AI Triage Gaps: The current software provides appointment booking forms but lacks
         an intelligent system that can advise patients on how to select the correct department or
         doctor.

    · Push Notification Gaps: Current hospital software relies on the pull method; patients
         need to login to check any notifications. There is a need for push notification software
         where appointment booking and payment reminders are sent to the patients' email
         addresses.

    · Analytics Gaps: Hospital administrators using current software lack a dashboard that
         would give insights into patient flows, doctor efficiency, and department load.

    · Integration Gaps: Scheduling, billing, medical record keeping, and pharmacy
         management are independent systems.

2.5 Solution to Bridge the Gap

The UniCare Hospital Management System caters to these weaknesses by leveraging the
strength of MySQL, powerful Node.js, advanced Google Gemini AI, and active communication
by Nodemailer. The UniCare HMS helps create a smart hospital ecosystem through the
combination of these technologies, transforming the ordinary hospital portal into a cognitive
assistant, which takes care of everything from triage to notifications, all on one platform.

                     Proposed work and Methodology

4.1 Introduction

The objective of this project is to create a smart, automated and AI-enabled hospital management
system. UniCare HMS stands out in that it is not a typical hospital portal, but rather one which
is predictive and analytic. This process of creation ensures that the project develops logically
from conceptualization all the way to implementation.

4.2 Software Development Methodology (Iterative Model)

This model was applied in the current work to facilitate the development process through small
steps:

    · Planning and Requirement Analysis: The main features such as patient registration,
         appointment booking, and physician login were determined initially.

    · Core Module Development: The basic functionalities involving creation, reading,
         updating, and deleting patient and appointment information were implemented using
         Node.js and MySQL technologies.

    · Integration of AI and Notifications: Google Gemini AI (symptom evaluation) and
         Nodemailer notifications (email alerts) were incorporated into the stable core module.

    · Testing and Debugging: Each step was tested for errors in API calls, database queries,
         and sessions.

4.3 Proposed System Architecture Workflow

The design of this system follows a layered approach involving frontend, backend, and AI
services. The steps involved are:

Step 1: User Authentication: Admin, Doctor, or Patient logs into the system based on their
roles through the role-based authentication login process. Express-session is used for managing
sessions.

Step 2: Patient Symptom Entry (AI Process): Patients enter their symptoms in plain text. The
backend creates a structured input request for Google Gemini Pro and receives probable
conditions, suggested specialty department, and priority.

Step 3: Booking an Appointment: The patient chooses a doctor from the suggested specialty
department and schedules an appointment. The backend checks the doctor's availability from
the MySQL database and verifies the appointment scheduling.

17
Step 4: Email Confirmation: An automated confirmation email is sent by Nodemailer to the
patient confirming their appointment details such as the doctor, date, time, and OPD room
number.

Step 5 ­ Doctor's Portal: The doctor signs in to check appointments, patient medical history,
diagnose diseases, and create prescriptions online.

Step 6 ­ Billing and Analytics: The administrator creates invoices for consultations and
treatments. The analytical panel provides key performance indicators like patient numbers,
income, and booking statuses.

4.4 Modular Description

The enable the code to be easily maintainable and scalable by splitting it into four main modules:

4.4.1 Admin Management Module

This is the control center of the entire system.

It comprises CRUD functionalities for patients, doctors, and departments, file uploading for labs
and prescriptions with Multer middleware, and a read analytics dashboard with information
regarding appointment details, revenue details, and doctor details.

4.4.2 Student Dashboard Module
End-user module that is personalized.

This includes AI Symptom Checker, appointment booking facility with slot availability, viewing
medical history (diagnosis & prescriptions), and downloading bills in PDF format.

4.4.3 AI Intelligence (Gemini Integration)

Doctor-specific perspective. Includes the list of appointments today with patient details,
prescription writer, updating patient medical records after consultation, and full patient history
from the assigned patient list.

4.4.4 Email Module (Nodemailer)

This is the "brain" of the entire system. Natural Language Processing via Gemini Pro API
processes the patient symptoms. Prompt Engineering makes sure that the responses are action-
oriented with probable diagnosis, department suggestions, and urgency levels mentioned.

4.5 Technical Implementation Logic

    · Password Hashing: bcryptjs is utilized for hashing passwords with salts. No plain text
         passwords are ever stored.

    · Session Handling: express-session manages authentication sessions with role
         restrictions.

18
    · Asynchronous Functions: All calls to AI APIs and email sending functions are
         asynchronous to avoid delay experiences for users.

    · Data Consistency: Foreign key relationships between appointments, patient records,
         and invoices in MySQL ensure cascading deletes maintain consistency.

4.6 Approach to Improving Performance

    · Cache AI Answers: Results of symptom analysis are stored in the database after the
         first request and pulled from there on future visits, which saves on API expenses and
         speeds up answers.

    · Responsive User Interface: CSS Flexbox and Media Queries guarantee that the UI is
         as functional on a 6-inch phone screen as on a 27-inch one.

                         Testing and Implementation

6.1 Understanding Software Testing

Testing is a vital phase in the SDLC that ensures the system's security, functionality, and
effectiveness. In this case, considering that this is an important system that requires high-quality
software, testing takes place at the point where creation meets implementation. This process is
not simply about identifying flaws; rather, it involves ensuring that all necessary functions are
carried out efficiently. From basic operations like creating, reading, updating, and deleting data
to advanced functionalities like analyzing symptoms through artificial intelligence and sending
automatic emails, everything should be executed perfectly.

6.2 Testing Strategies and Methodologies

To develop quality software, several testing techniques were adopted. These testing techniques
can be employed to perform tests on the system at a high level as well as the code level.

6.2.1 Black Box Testing
In this approach, the code used in the system was considered as a "black box" and there was no
interest other than the input and output. Testing user interaction was performed to make sure
that the system works as expected. As an example, testing the sign-in process using valid or
invalid logins for all three users was done. End-to-end testing of appointment booking was
performed from entering symptoms until receiving the booking email.

6.2.2 White Box Testing
The testing was implemented for the backend using Node.js and Express. Focus was placed on
path testing, which guarantees correct execution of each branch of the code. Special focus was
also put on handling asynchronous functions, especially those using the Gemini AI API and
Nodemailer to prevent server crash due to API timeout and SMTP errors.

6.3 Various Levels of Testing

6.3.1 Module Level Testing
    · Authentication Unit: Session management was verified to make sure that patients could
         not have access to doctors' and admins' routes without appropriate role-based
         authentication.
    · Appointment Management Unit: The appointment's CRUD functionalities were
         tested, along with special cases like double booking and future date verification.

27
    · AI Engine Unit: A range of symptoms with increasing difficulty was sent to the Gemini
         API to check if it provided structured results in reasonable time frames.

    · Billing Unit: The invoice creation process was tested for various types of consultations,
         treatments, and pharmacy items.

6.3.2 Integration Testing
    · Back-end to Database Integration: All appointments made, record modifications, and
         billing activities were checked to ensure that data persisted properly in MySQL.

patients receive notifications actively), resulting in lower missed appointments and delayed
payments.

databases.

8.1 Conclusion

UniCare Hospital Management System can be termed a successful transition from using manual
approaches towards paper-based, scattered hospital management towards a consolidated,
digitalized AI-enabled platform. Indeed, the aims of ensuring accurate and reliable data being
provided to all relevant parties have been accomplished through implementing the MEEN Stack
(MySQL, Express.js, Gemini AI, Node.js).

Specifically, through incorporation of Google Gemini AI, UniCare Hospital Management
System transcends the status of simple appointment management and becomes an AI-enabled
clinical triage and guidance platform that helps schedule and guide patients to specialists. The
use of AI-based symptom checker resolves the problem of incorrect routing of patients, which
often occurs because there is no clinical advice at the point of access.

Automated emails through Nodemailer and separate dashboards for Admin, Doctor, and Patient
introduce additional levels of accountability that are completely missing in the existing manual
system. Technically, UniCare HMS proves itself to be a reliable and economically viable
solution for educational institution-affiliated and tier-2 cities hospitals.

8.2 Future Work

8.2.1 Native Mobile Application

This system is currently built using a responsive web application approach. The natural
progression from here would be to build native mobile apps, either on the Android OS (using
frameworks like React Native or Flutter), where push notifications can be utilized, which are
more effective than emails.

8.2.2 Telemedicine Integration

A further development of this software may involve the addition of a video consultation module
based on WebRTC, through which patients will be able to have an online discussion with their
respective doctors without making any trips.

8.2.3 Multilingual AI Support

India is a country that is multilingual. In a future version of the software, the services provided
by the Gemini API translation platform can be used to offer symptom assessment and booking
alerts in Hindi, Marathi, Tamil, or any other local language.

8.2.4 Integrated Pharmacy and Inventory Module

The inclusion of the Pharmacy and Medical Inventory Module will enable the software to
automatically create prescription order fulfillment, keep track of the quantity of medicines, alert
the admin in case of low stock, and prepare the pharmacy bill based on the patient's prescription.

8.2.5 Integration with National Health Stack (NHA)

Connecting UniCare HMS with other NHA services like the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission
(ABDM) and the Unified Health Interface (UHI) will permit patients to link their ABHA
account ID through which they can share records from one hospital to another.

8.2.6 Predictive Analytics and AI Diagnostics

With larger companies, it would be possible to train machine learning models using past patient
records to forecast trends regarding diseases, detect patients who are at risk (such as those likely
to require readmission) and suggest preventive measures that can help turn UniCare HMS into
a proactive system.

8.3 Limitations

    · Internet Dependency: Since the system operates online, it is dependent on reliable
         internet connectivity for functions like booking appointments, AI symptom checker, and
         emails.

    · Inadequate AI Precision: While the AI Symptom Checker offers suggestions from
         patient input, it can never substitute a medical expert's judgment and may not always
         offer 100 percent precision.

    · Non-functionality in Offline Mode: There is no offline mode, which might pose
         difficulties where there are low network connections.

    · Lack of SMS or Push Notifications: At the moment, notification through email is the
         sole method available in the system; SMS or push notifications have yet to be
         introduced.

    · Hospital Capacity: It has been optimized for small-to-medium hospitals; hence,
         handling large hospital data would be challenging.

    · Security Features: While there are security features such as password hashing and input
         validation, there are none like biometrics or multi-factor authentication.

    · Mobile App Absence: The system can only be accessed via a web browser.
